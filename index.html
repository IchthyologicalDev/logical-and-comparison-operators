Logical and Comparison Operators Exercise

Description

Welcome to the logical and comparison operators exercise! You'll get to practice writing functions that use some common logical and comparison operators. Good luck!

Instructions

  1. Open up index.js in your editor.
  2. Follow the instructions in the comment at the top of that file.
  3. Refresh this page after saving changes to see your progress.

Questions

I catch a fish when it's rainy or it's been more than a week since the last time I caught a fish. Will I catch a fish today?

Ocean fishing can be exciting. You don't always know what you'll catch. I think that I caught a record breaking cod, but I'm not sure if it's actually a cod, or if it even broke the previous record of 103 pounds. Is this a record breaking cod?

My boat is only safe when the wind is less than 5 knots. Can my boat handle the wind?

Some fisherman can be really competitive. Present company excluded. Fisherman don't have fun if they haven't caught a fish, but someone else has. Did I have fun fishing with a friend?

Sharks will occasionally bite a hook that has an equal size or smaller fish on it. Our boat can handle any size fish except for sharks. Sharks must be smaller than our boat. Will a shark bite, and is our boat big enough?

I'm willing to move fishing locations if I haven't caught a fish in a while, and the new location is closer to shore. Should I move?

I can justify fishing when it's a weekend or when my work backlog is empty. Can I justify fishing today?

I like catch and release fishing. Sometimes I wonder if I've ever caught the same fish twice. Is this the same fish?
